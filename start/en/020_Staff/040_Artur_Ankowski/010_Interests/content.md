## Neutrino interactions with atomic nuclei

**Overarching goal**: understanding the asymmetry between matter and antimatter in the Universe

Equal amounts of matter and antimatter were created in the Big Bang. 
However, the evolution of the Universe generated a tiny imbalance: for every 
1010 particles of antimatter, there were 1010 + 1 particles of matter. We know that this 
difference does not originate from quarks.

**Crucial step**: Measurement of the asymmetry between neutrinos and antineutrinos

Neutrinos are neutral particles whose interactions can produce electrons, muons, 
and tauons. We distinguish three types of neutrinos, depending on which particles 
they produce. However, the type of neutrino can change over time through a 
process known as oscillations. Since neutrinos and antineutrinos may oscillate differently, 
they could be the very reason for the dominance of matter over antimatter in the Universe.

**Prerequisite**: Accurate modeling of neutrino interactions with atomic nuclei

More than 1,800 physicists at the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (USA) and 
Hyper-Kamiokande (Japan) are currently preparing precision measurements of 
neutrino and antineutrino oscillations. Their success depends on the accurate modeling of 
neutrino and antineutrino interactions with atomic nuclei, which requires a 
significant advancement in our knowledge.
